The Pantone selection is a deliberative process based on trend analysis, not a random draw. Key drivers include:

2.  **Zeitgeist Reflection (Large):** The core of Pantone's stated mission. The color aims to capture the global mood. Current themes of technological disruption (AI), environmental concern, and economic anxiety are primary inputs.
3.  **Commercial Applicability (Moderate):** The chosen color must be viable for partners in fashion, graphic design, and interior decorating. This disfavors overly niche or difficult-to-use colors and makes foundational colors like Black or White unlikely to be the sole "Color of the Year."
4.  **Aspirational Messaging (Moderate):** The choice is often forward-looking, meant to inspire a particular feeling for the year ahead (e.g., optimism, calm, creativity). The narrative is as important as the color itself.
5.  **Novelty and Surprise (Small):** While reflecting trends, Pantone also aims to *set* them. This can lead to less obvious choices, such as creating a new color for the first time (2022's Very Peri) or selecting a dual color.
